Astronomy Background

Light & Observation
The Electromagnetic Spectrum
Astronomical objects emit radiation across the full EM spectrum. SDSS observes in optical and near-IR — understanding why requires knowing what each band reveals about temperature, composition, and distance.
Coordinate Systems
Right Ascension & Declination
The celestial sphere uses RA (α) and Dec (δ) analogous to longitude and latitude. Every SDSS object carries these columns. Galactic coordinates (l, b) are used for Milky Way science to track dust latitude.
Distances
Redshift, z, and Cosmological Distance
Redshift z = (λobs − λrest) / λrest encodes recession velocity and lookback time. Luminosity distance and comoving distance require a cosmological model (ΛCDM); astropy.cosmology handles this.
Brightness
Magnitudes, Flux & the AB System
SDSS uses AB magnitudes calibrated to a flat-spectrum source. m = −2.5 log₁₀(fν) + 48.6. Brighter objects have lower magnitudes. Differences of 5 mag = factor of 100 in flux.
Stars
Stellar Classification: OBAFGKM
Spectral types correlate with surface temperature and color. The Hertzsprung-Russell diagram organizes stars by luminosity vs. temperature — the single most important diagram in all of stellar astronomy.
Survey Design
Why Build a Survey?
Individual observations are anecdotal; surveys are statistical. SDSS's billion-object catalog enables population studies, rare-object discovery, and cosmological inference impossible with pointed observations alone.

SDSS Data Architecture

Survey History
SDSS Generations: DR1 → DR19
SDSS-I (2000–2005) imaged a quarter of the sky. Each successive phase — SDSS-II, III, IV, V — added new spectrographs, targets, and survey programs. DR17 is the final SDSS-IV release; DR19 (SDSS-V) is current.
Telescope
The 2.5m at Apache Point Observatory
Located at 9,000 ft in the Sacramento Mountains, NM. Drift-scan imaging in five bands with a 120-megapixel mosaic CCD camera (retired 2009). Now spectroscopy-only with 640-fiber spectrographs.
Access Portals
SkyServer & CasJobs
SkyServer (skyserver.sdss.org) provides a web SQL interface and object explorer. CasJobs allows long-running queries, result storage, and cross-matching of uploaded tables against the full SDSS catalog.
Sub-Surveys
Legacy, BOSS, eBOSS, MaNGA, APOGEE
Each program targets different science: Legacy mapped galaxy clustering; BOSS detected BAO; MaNGA mapped galaxy interiors; APOGEE surveyed Milky Way stars in infrared. Data lives in different tables and schemas.

Your First SDSS Query

Paste this into SkyServer SQL Search or run it in CasJobs to retrieve a clean photometric sample around a target coordinate.

SQL · CasJobs / SkyServer week1_cone_search.sql 
-- Cone search: galaxies within 30 arcmin of M51 (Whirlpool Galaxy)
-- Returns clean photometry with u,g,r,i,z model magnitudes

SELECT TOP 500
    p.objID,
    p.ra,
    p.dec,
    p.modelMag_u AS u,
    p.modelMag_g AS g,
    p.modelMag_r AS r,
    p.modelMag_i AS i,
    p.modelMag_z AS z,
    p.petroRad_r,
    p.type          -- 3 = galaxy, 6 = star
FROM
    PhotoObj AS p
WHERE
    -- dbo.fGetNearbyObjEq(ra, dec, radius_arcmin)
    p.objID IN (
        SELECT objID FROM dbo.fGetNearbyObjEq(202.4696, 47.1952, 30)
    )
    AND p.type = 3                    -- galaxies only
    AND p.clean = 1                   -- clean photometry flag
    AND p.modelMag_r BETWEEN 14 AND 22  -- reasonable magnitude range
ORDER BY
    p.modelMag_r ASC

Jupyter Notebook Exercise

📓 week1_sdss_intro.ipynb

This notebook introduces the SDSS data ecosystem end-to-end. By the end you will have written a working SQL query, downloaded results into pandas, and produced a publication-style color-color diagram separating stars from galaxies.

  1. 01 Set up your environment on SciServer Compute. Import astropy, pandas, matplotlib, and the SciServer Python library.
  2. 02 Submit the cone-search SQL above via SciServer.CasJobs.executeQuery() and load results into a DataFrame.
  3. 03 Plot a u−g vs. g−r color-color diagram. Overplot the theoretical stellar locus in a contrasting color. Identify the galaxy cloud vs. star locus.
  4. 04 Use astropy.coordinates.SkyCoord to convert your RA/Dec results to galactic coordinates (l, b) and plot the sky footprint.
  5. 05 Write a brief (<200 word) markdown cell explaining what the stellar locus is physically, and why galaxies scatter off it in color-color space.
